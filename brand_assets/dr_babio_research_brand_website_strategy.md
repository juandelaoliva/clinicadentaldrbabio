**Prepared for:** Juan de la Oliva  
**Prepared on:** 2026-04-20  
**Language:** English  
**Scope:** Consolidated public-internet research to support a website proposal, content planning, and an initial brand guideline for **Clínica Dental Dr. Babío / Clínica Dental Doctor Babío / Dental Babio SLP**.

---

## 1) Executive summary

This is **not** a zero-brand clinic. It is a clinic with a **real local footprint**, a **long operating history**, and signs of **family continuity**, but with a **fragmented and weak digital presence**.

The public footprint suggests:

- A long-established dental practice in Seville operating from **C. Canal, 2, 1ºL, 41006 Sevilla**.
- Strong association with **Raúl Babio Bernal** and later also **Raúl Babio Arjona**.
- Public healthcare-directory evidence of dental authorization dating back to **1997**.
- A likely recent legal/professional restructuring under **Dental Babio SLP** in **2025**.
- A digital presence spread across directories, Facebook traces, and legacy references to a website/domain, but **no clearly functioning, current official website surfaced during this research**.

### Strategic conclusion

The opportunity is clear: the website proposal should **not invent a brand from scratch**. It should **organize, modernize, and clarify** an existing reputation.

The best positioning is not “high-tech dentistry” or “cheap dentistry.” Those are generic claims in the market. The strongest angle is:

> **An established, trusted, family-linked dental clinic in Seville, combining experience, proximity, and professional continuity.**

## 2) Research methodology and confidence model

### What this report includes

This document consolidates public signals from:

- business directories
- healthcare directories
- company databases
- public Facebook search snippets
- address/contact listings
- legal/mercantile references

### What this report does **not** include

- direct access to the clinic owners
- Google Business Profile admin data
- verified first-party analytics
- verified internal treatment portfolio
- verified current opening hours from the clinic itself
- verified current branding files or logos

### Confidence levels used in this document

- **Confirmed:** repeated across multiple public sources or supported by structured business/legal listings.
- **Probable:** appears in public sources but needs first-party confirmation before publication.
- **Unverified / should not be published as fact yet:** present in low-trust or derivative directories, inconsistent across sources, or impossible to validate here.

## 7) Strategic brand diagnosis

## 7.1 The strongest brand assets already present

Based on the public footprint, the strongest assets are:

1. **Longevity** — dental authorization signal dating to 1997.
2. **Local stability** — consistent address in Seville across sources.
3. **Family/professional continuity** — Babio Bernal + Babio Arjona under Dental Babio SLP.
4. **Human scale** — team signals are person-based, not corporate.
5. **Trust potential** — healthcare legitimacy and continuity are much more credible than fashionable marketing claims.

## 7.2 The weakest areas

1. **Naming fragmentation**
2. **No clear first-party digital home**
3. **Inconsistent public data**
4. **No controlled storytelling**
5. **No strong visual system visible in public sources**
6. **Potential underuse of local SEO and reputation management**

## 7.3 Recommended core positioning

### Recommended positioning statement

**A long-established dental clinic in Seville, built on experience, close patient care, and professional continuity.**

### Recommended brand promise

**Clear, trustworthy dental care delivered by a clinic that values long-term relationships, calm guidance, and quality treatment.**

### Recommended differentiators

- Established local presence
- Real people, real continuity
- Private clinic with professional credibility
- Dental care that feels personal, not industrial

### What to avoid

Avoid generic positioning like:

- “latest technology” as a central message
- “best clinic in Seville” style claims
- low-price messaging unless pricing strategy is explicit
- franchise-like visuals or stock-photo branding
- exaggerated luxury positioning

My opinion: this brand should feel **solid, calm, credible, and close**, not flashy.

## 8) Tone of voice recommendation

## 8.1 Recommended tone

- **Clear**
- **Reassuring**
- **Professional**
- **Warm but not sentimental**
- **Direct and easy to understand**
- **Local and human**

## 8.2 Tone characteristics

### Use language that sounds:

- experienced
- calm
- respectful
- precise
- understandable for non-specialists

### Avoid language that sounds:

- over-marketed
- salesy
- technical for the sake of sounding expert
- cold and corporate
- childish or gimmicky

## 8.3 Tone examples

### Good direction

- “We explain each treatment clearly.”
- “We want you to feel informed and confident before starting.”
- “Our clinic combines experience, continuity, and close patient care.”
- “We care about long-term oral health, not rushed decisions.”

### Bad direction

- “State-of-the-art smile transformations.”
- “Elite cosmetic dental excellence.”
- “The most innovative dentistry in Seville.”
- “Luxury smile design experiences.”

### Tone summary in one sentence

**Experienced, human dentistry explained with clarity and calm authority.**

## 9) Initial brand guideline recommendation

## 9.1 Brand personality

The clinic should feel:

- **Trustworthy**
- **Established**
- **Approachable**
- **Professional**
- **Serene**

## 9.2 Visual direction

### Recommended visual mood

- clean
- bright
- calm
- real
- understated

### Recommended visual strategy

- use **real photography** of clinic, reception, treatment spaces, and team
- avoid glossy stock photography of perfect-model smiles
- prefer candid, clean, professional portraits
- use whitespace generously
- use simple layouts with high readability

## 9.3 Color direction

Recommended palette direction:

- **Primary:** deep muted blue, blue-grey, or dark teal
- **Secondary:** warm white / soft off-white
- **Neutrals:** light grey, stone, soft charcoal
- **Accent:** restrained sage or muted green if needed

### Why

This supports trust, hygiene, calm, and maturity without looking cold.

## 9.4 Typography direction

- one clean, modern sans-serif for body and UI
- optionally one slightly more classic type treatment for headlines if it still feels clinical and readable
- never overly decorative

## 9.5 Imagery principles

Show:

- team introductions
- patient journey spaces
- consultation environments
- instruments/clinical setting only in moderation
- neighborhood/locality context when useful

Do not overuse:

- extreme close-up dental imagery
- intimidating procedure images
- generic stock smiling faces
